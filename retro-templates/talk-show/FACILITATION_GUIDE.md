# The Late Night Talk Show - Facilitation Guide

**Facilitator Role:** Show Host
**Duration:** 55 minutes
**Format:** Talk show with monologue, guest segments, audience interaction

> **Note:** How much you lean into the talk show character is entirely up to the facilitator's taste. Go full late-night host or keep it subtle - whatever feels right for your team.

---

## Preparation (before the retro, ~10 min)

1. **Choose the theme** - Pick a talk show flavor for this sprint (classic late night, morning show, podcast, etc.)
2. **Prepare the template on the whiteboard** - Import the SVG template, lock the background layer so participants don't accidentally move it
3. **Pre-fill sprint metrics** - Pull sprint stats from the Sprint Review and fill in the "Ratings" section (velocity, completed vs planned, carry-over, etc.)
4. **Review section ownership** - Familiarize yourself with which sections you fill vs. the team (see below)

---

## Setup

- Print SVG templates (one per participant) OR set up digital whiteboard
- Markers, pens
- Timer visible to all
- Optional: desk setup, mug as prop, "ON AIR" sign, intro music

---

## Section Ownership

### Facilitator fills before the retro (from Sprint Review)
- **Ratings** - Sprint stats and metrics (velocity, story points, bugs, etc.)
- **Show date / season info** - Sprint number, date range, season/episode numbering

### Facilitator fills during writing phase (from ice breaker data) — Phase 3
- **Studio Vibe** - Mood/genre collected from the team
- **Shoutout Award** - Kudos collected from the team
- **Quotable Moment** - Highlight reel quote collected from the team

### Team fills together
- **Show name** and **catchphrase** - Decided collaboratively during Set the Stage

### Participants fill individually
- **Opening Monologue** - Sprint summary with your best material
- **Tonight's Guest** - Spotlight a feature or project, interview-style
- **Desk Bit** - Recurring segment, technical deep-dive with flair
- **Audience Question** - Something you genuinely want to discuss
- **Top 5 List** - Countdown of sprint moments
- **Fan Mail** - Feedback, praise, or questions from stakeholders/users

---

## Phase 1: Set the Stage (0:00-0:05, 5 min)

### Show Host Script:

> "Gooood [morning/afternoon], everybody! Welcome, welcome to [The Sprint Show]! We've got a GREAT show for you today!
>
> We've had quite a sprint, folks. [Optional: one-liner about something that happened]. But we'll get to all that.
>
> First things first - what's our show called? And what's our catchphrase?"

*Wait for the team to decide on a show name and catchphrase, then fill those in on the board.*

> "Love it. Now, here's how tonight's show works. Some sections are already filled in - our ratings, the season info. During the next few minutes I'll collect a few things from all of you. Then you'll each prepare your own segments:
> - An **opening monologue**, a **tonight's guest** segment, a **desk bit**, an **audience question**, a **top 5 list**, or **fan mail**.
>
> Let's get into it!"

---

## Phase 2: Ice Breakers (0:05-0:12, 7 min)

Collect from ALL participants and note down responses. Do NOT fill template sections yet - you'll do that during the writing phase.

1. **Studio Vibe** - "If this sprint were a TV show, what genre would it be? Comedy, drama, thriller, documentary?"
2. **Shoutout Award** - "You're handing out a trophy tonight. Who gets it and what's engraved on it?"
3. **Quotable Moment** - "What's the line from this sprint that would make the highlight reel?"

---

## Phase 3: Individual Writing (0:12-0:24, 12 min)

### Prompts:

- "If a talk show host interviewed you about this sprint, what's the story you'd tell?"
- "What moment from this sprint would get the biggest laugh or gasp from the audience?"
- "What happened this sprint that deserves its own segment?"
- "When did you feel like a star, or when did you want to hide backstage?"
- "What clip from this sprint would go viral?"

### Facilitator actions:

- Fill the ice breaker sections on the board from your notes (Studio Vibe, Shoutout Award, Quotable Moment) - add flair, tidy up wording
- Polish the pre-filled sections you own (Ratings, show date/season info)
- "Writers' room is closing in 5 minutes!"
- "Final joke polish - 2 minutes, people!"

---

## Phase 4: Gallery Walk (0:24-0:29, 5 min)

> "Aaand we're on a commercial break! Use this time to walk around and preview tonight's lineup. No spoilers - just read."

---

## Phase 5: Sharing (0:29-0:40, 11 min)

Each person gets ~1.5 minutes.

Stay in character: "What a segment! The audience loved that!" or "Oooh, controversial. Let's discuss after the break."

---

## Phase 6: Discussion (0:40-0:50, 10 min)

Pick 2-3:

**On the show:**
- "Which segment would get the most views if we posted it?"
- "If this sprint were a sitcom, what would the episode title be?"
- "What was the plot twist nobody saw coming?"

**Behind the scenes:**
- "What happened backstage that the audience didn't see?"
- "If we could rewrite one scene, what would it be?"
- "What running gag do we need to retire?"

**Looking ahead:**
- "What's the teaser for next week's episode?"
- "Who should we book as next sprint's guest star?"

---

## Phase 7: Closing (0:50-0:55, 5 min)

> "That's our show for today, folks! You've been a wonderful audience. Don't forget to tip your scrum master. See you next sprint - same time, same channel!"

- Capture 1-3 action items
- Optional: **Exit mood** - quick round of "one word for how you're leaving tonight's show"
- Optional: **Vote for Best Segment** - team picks the MVP segment of the night

---

## Post-Retro

- Compile into show recap
- Share: "ICYMI: [Show Name] Season [X] Episode [XX] - Now streaming!"
