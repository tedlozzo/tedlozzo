# The Newspaper - Facilitation Guide

**Facilitator Role:** Editor-in-Chief
**Duration:** 55 minutes
**Format:** Team newspaper with articles, headlines, and classifieds

---

## Preparation (Before the Retro, ~10 min)

1. **Choose the theme** - Pick a newspaper style or tone that fits the sprint (tabloid, broadsheet, local gazette, etc.). This sets the vibe for the whole session.
2. **Prepare the template** - Import the SVG into Miro/Mural, resize to fit, and **lock the template layer** so participants don't accidentally move it.
3. **Pre-fill sprint metrics** - Pull data from the Sprint Review and fill in the **Sports & Scores** section: velocity, stories completed, bugs fixed, sprint goal status, etc.
4. **Review section ownership** - Familiarize yourself with which sections you own vs. which the team fills (see Section Ownership below). Have your content ready for the date line and sprint number.

---

## Setup

- Set up digital whiteboard with locked SVG template OR print templates (one per participant)
- Markers, pens, stickers
- Timer visible to all
- Optional: newsroom ambiance music, press badge props

---

## Section Ownership

| Responsibility | Sections | When |
|---|---|---|
| **Facilitator fills before retro** (from Sprint Review) | Sports & Scores (sprint metrics), date line, sprint number | During Preparation |
| **Facilitator fills during writing phase** (from ice breaker data) | Weather (mood), Kudos (shoutouts), Quote of the Sprint | Phase 3 |
| **Team fills together** | Masthead (newspaper name), Coming Soon | Phase 1 |
| **Participants fill individually** | Front Page, Feature, Tech Corner, Opinion, In Brief, Classifieds, Comics | Phase 3 |

---

## Phase 1: Set the Stage (0:00-0:05, 5 min)

### Editor-in-Chief Script:

> "Good [morning/afternoon], reporters. Welcome to the newsroom. I'm your Editor-in-Chief, and we've got a paper to put out.
>
> This sprint generated a lot of stories, and I need YOUR reporting. Each of you is going to write one or more articles for our newspaper. Pick your section - it can be:
> - A **front page story** (the big thing that happened)
> - A **feature article** (deep dive on something you worked on)
> - A **tech corner piece** (something technical worth sharing)
> - An **opinion column** (your honest take)
> - A **classified ad** (something you need or can offer)
> - A **brief** (a quick note worth mentioning)
> - A **comic** (a funny moment, drawn or described)
>
> But first - we need a name for our paper. Any suggestions?"

Let the team vote on a newspaper name. Write it on the masthead. Briefly explain which sections they'll fill individually vs. what's already been prepared.

---

## Phase 2: Ice Breakers (0:05-0:12, 7 min)

Go around the room and collect from ALL participants:

1. **Mood Weather** - "In one word, what's the weather in your world right now? Sunny, cloudy, stormy, foggy?"
2. **Shoutout** - "Name one person and one thing they did this sprint that deserves recognition."
3. **Quote of the Sprint** - "What's the most memorable thing someone said these past two weeks?"

As people share, the facilitator collects and notes down all responses — the **Weather**, **Kudos**, and **Quote of the Sprint** template sections get filled during the writing phase (Phase 3). This keeps you focused on the room rather than editing the board live.

---

## Phase 3: Individual Writing (0:12-0:24, 12 min)

### Prompts to share:

- "If a journalist followed our team this sprint, what story would they write about?"
- "What story from this sprint deserves to be told but might otherwise be forgotten?"
- "What happened this sprint that matters?"
- "When did you feel most proud or most frustrated these past two weeks?"
- "What moment from this sprint would you want other teams to know about?"

### Facilitator actions:

- Start timer
- Give 5-minute and 2-minute warnings
- Walk around but don't interrupt
- "Reporters, 2 minutes to deadline!"
- **Fill the ice breaker sections now** - This is when you transfer your notes into the **Weather**, **Kudos**, and **Quote of the Sprint** sections on the template. Much more efficient than editing live in front of the audience. Also finalize **Coming Soon** with any sprint-ahead items you know about

---

## Phase 4: Gallery Walk (0:24-0:29, 5 min)

> "Time's up, pencils down! Now we do a silent gallery walk. Stand up, read everyone's articles. No talking - just absorb."

Ensure all articles are visible. Note patterns for discussion.

---

## Phase 5: Headline Sharing (0:29-0:40, 11 min)

Each person gets ~1-1.5 minutes:
- Read their headline
- 30-second summary
- One thing they want the team to know

Stay in character: "Great scoop! Next reporter..." (The level of character play is up to the facilitator's taste and read of the room — go full Editor-in-Chief or keep it subtle.)

---

## Phase 6: Discussion (0:40-0:50, 10 min)

Pick 2-3 questions:

**On the stories:**
- "Which headline surprised you most?"
- "If you were the editor, which story goes above the fold?"
- "Did anyone cover the same event from a different angle?"

**On what's missing:**
- "Any stories that should have made the paper but didn't?"
- "What's the investigative piece we need to write about our process?"
- "If there was a corrections section, what would be in it?"

**Looking ahead:**
- "What's the headline we WANT to write next sprint?"
- "What section needs more coverage next time?"

---

## Phase 7: Closing (0:50-0:55, 5 min)

> "And that's a wrap on this edition! Remember - every sprint is a new issue. Let's make the next one front-page worthy."

Capture 1-3 action items, then pick one or more optional closing activities:

- **Exit mood** - "Give me your weather again. Has it changed since the start?"
- **One-word takeaway** - "One word that sums up this retro for you."
- **Best Headline vote** - Everyone votes for their favorite headline (not their own). Announce the winner with fanfare.

---

## Post-Retro

- Photograph all articles
- Compile into a team newspaper (PDF or Slack post)
- Share with: "EXTRA! EXTRA! Read all about Sprint [X]!"
