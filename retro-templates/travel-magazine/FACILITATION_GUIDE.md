# The Travel Magazine - Facilitation Guide

**Facilitator Role:** Travel Editor / Tour Guide
**Duration:** 55 minutes
**Format:** Travel magazine with destination reviews, postcards, and travel tips

**Note:** How much you lean into the Travel Editor character is entirely up to your taste. Go full theatrical or keep it subtle - whatever feels natural for your team.

---

## Preparation (before the retro, ~10 min)

1. **Choose the theme** - Pick a travel theme or let the sprint inspire one (e.g., "Jungle Expedition," "Mediterranean Cruise," "Road Trip")
2. **Prepare the template on your whiteboard** - Import the SVG template, lock it so participants don't accidentally move it
3. **Pre-fill sprint metrics** - Pull data from the Sprint Review and fill in the "Trip Stats" / metrics section (velocity, stories completed, bugs fixed, etc.)
4. **Fill in issue date/number** - Update the magazine issue number and date
5. **Review section ownership** - Know which sections you fill vs. the team (see below)

---

## Setup

- Print SVG templates (one per participant) OR set up digital whiteboard
- Markers, pens, stickers
- Timer visible to all
- Optional: travel props (small globe, sunglasses, boarding pass printouts)

---

## Section Ownership

### Facilitator fills before the retro (from Sprint Review)

- **Trip Stats** - Sprint metrics (velocity, completed stories, bugs, etc.)
- **Issue date/number** - Magazine issue and sprint date range

### Facilitator fills during writing phase (from ice breaker data) (Phase 3)

- **Weather at Destination** - Mood collected from participants
- **Travel Companion Award** - Kudos nomination from the team
- **Overheard at the Airport** - Memorable quote from the sprint

### Team fills together (Phase 1)

- **Magazine name and motto** - Collaborative naming of the issue

### Participants fill individually (Phase 3)

- **Destination Review** - Main feature or project explored
- **Postcard Home** - Quick update to stakeholders
- **Travel Tip** - Lesson learned for future travelers
- **Off the Beaten Path** - Something unexpected discovered
- **Delayed Flight** - What got stuck, cancelled, or rerouted
- **Packing List** - Action items to carry forward

---

## Phase 1: Set the Stage (0:00-0:05, 5 min)

### Travel Editor Script:

> "Welcome aboard, fellow travelers! This sprint has been quite the journey, and it's time to write about our adventures for the next issue of our magazine.
>
> Each of you is a travel writer covering a different angle of our trip. You can write:
> - A **destination review** (the main feature or project you explored)
> - A **postcard home** (quick update to stakeholders, postcard style)
> - A **travel tip** (lesson learned that helps future travelers)
> - An **off the beaten path** piece (something unexpected you discovered)
> - A **delayed flight** report (what got stuck, cancelled, or rerouted)
>
> First things first - what's our magazine called? And what's our motto?"

Explain section ownership: some sections are already pre-filled with sprint data, some you will fill together during ice breakers, and the rest are for individual writing.

---

## Phase 2: Ice Breakers (0:05-0:12, 7 min)

Collect responses from ALL participants. The facilitator notes down answers (notebook, scratch pad, etc.) but does NOT fill template sections yet - that happens during the writing phase.

1. **Weather at Destination** - "What's the weather where your sprint took you? Tropical paradise, unexpected monsoon, clear skies, or lost in fog?"
2. **Travel Companion Award** - "Who was the best travel buddy this sprint? Name them and say why."
3. **Overheard at the Airport** - "What's the most memorable thing someone said this sprint? The thing you'd overhear and chuckle about."

---

## Phase 3: Individual Writing (0:12-0:24, 12 min)

### Prompts:

- "If a travel writer followed our team this sprint, what destination review would they write?"
- "What hidden gem did you discover this sprint that others might have missed?"
- "What part of the journey matters most to remember?"
- "When did the trip feel most exciting, and when did you feel most lost?"
- "What postcard would you send to another team about this sprint?"

### Facilitator actions:

- While the team writes, fill the ice breaker sections on the template from your notes (Weather, Kudos, Quote) and polish your pre-filled Trip Stats - tighten wording, add flair
- "Boarding closes in 5 minutes, writers!"
- "Last call! 2 minutes to filing deadline!"

---

## Phase 4: Gallery Walk (0:24-0:29, 5 min)

> "The in-flight entertainment for today: a reading tour. Walk around and read your fellow travelers' stories. No discussion yet - just soak it in."

---

## Phase 5: Sharing (0:29-0:40, 11 min)

Each person gets ~1.5 minutes to present.

Stay in character: "What a destination! Would you recommend it?" or "Sounds like that flight delay was rough. What happened?"

---

## Phase 6: Discussion (0:40-0:50, 10 min)

Pick 2-3:

**On the journey:**
- "Which destination surprised you most?"
- "If you had to write a 1-star review of something this sprint, what would it be?"
- "What hidden gem would you recommend to other teams?"

**On the route:**
- "Were we on the planned itinerary or did we go off-road?"
- "What should we pack differently next time?"
- "Which delayed flight cost us the most?"

**Looking ahead:**
- "Where should we travel next sprint?"
- "What's the one thing on our packing list we absolutely cannot forget?"

---

## Phase 7: Closing (0:50-0:55, 5 min)

> "And that wraps up this issue of our magazine! Pack your bags, recharge, and get ready for the next destination. The journey never stops - it just gets more interesting."

Capture 1-3 action items.

**Optional closing activities:**
- **Exit mood** - Quick round: "Rate your trip 1-5 stars" or "One word for how you feel leaving this retro"
- **Vote for Best Travel Story** - Each participant votes for their favorite contribution from another team member

---

## Post-Retro

- Compile into magazine issue
- Share: "New issue out! [Magazine Name], Issue [X] - now in your inbox!"
