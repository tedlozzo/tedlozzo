# The Detective Case File - Facilitation Guide

**Facilitator Role:** Chief Inspector / Lead Detective
**Duration:** 55 minutes
**Format:** Case file with witness statements, forensic analysis, and suspect profiles
**Character level:** How much you lean into the detective persona is entirely up to the facilitator's taste - from subtle flavor to full noir. Adjust to what feels right for your team.

---

## Preparation (~10 min before the retro)

1. **Choose the theme** - Pick a detective sub-theme if desired (noir, cold case, heist) or keep it classic
2. **Prepare the template** - Import the SVG onto the digital whiteboard and lock it in place
3. **Pre-fill Case Statistics** - Pull sprint metrics from the Sprint Review (velocity, completed vs planned, bugs, etc.) and fill in the "Case Statistics" section
4. **Fill case number and date** - Set the case number (e.g., sprint number) and date
5. **Review section ownership** - Familiarize yourself with which sections you fill vs. the team (see below)

---

## Setup

- Digital whiteboard with SVG template imported and locked
- Timer visible to all
- Optional: magnifying glass prop, detective hat, "CLASSIFIED" folder
- Have Sprint Review data handy for Case Statistics

---

## Section Ownership

### Facilitator fills before the retro (from Sprint Review)
- **Case Statistics** - Sprint metrics (velocity, stories completed, bugs, etc.)
- **Case number and date**

### Facilitator fills during Writing Phase (from Ice Breaker data)
- **Precinct Morale** - Team mood (collected in Phase 2, filled in Phase 3)
- **Commendations** - Kudos nominated by team (collected in Phase 2, filled in Phase 3)
- **Overheard in the Precinct** - Memorable quote from the sprint (collected in Phase 2, filled in Phase 3)

### Team fills together
- **Case file name** - The team names the case collaboratively

### Participants fill individually
- **Case Summary** - The main story of the sprint
- **Witness Statement** - Personal account ("I was there when...")
- **Forensic Analysis** - Technical deep-dive on evidence
- **Suspect Profile** - Root cause analysis
- **Cold Cases** - Unresolved items from previous sprints
- **Most Wanted** - Priority items for next sprint
- **Sketch Artist** - Visual representation or diagram

---

## Phase 1: Set the Stage (0:00-0:05, 5 min)

### Chief Inspector Script:

> "Attention, detectives. We've got a case to close. This sprint has generated several leads and I need your reports on my desk.
>
> First - what do we call this case? Every good investigation needs a name."

- Collect suggestions, pick one (or vote quickly)
- Briefly explain section ownership: which sections participants will fill individually, and which the facilitator handles

---

## Phase 2: Ice Breakers (0:05-0:12, 7 min)

Collect from ALL participants:

1. **Precinct Morale** - "What's the mood at headquarters? On high alert, calm and collected, battle-weary, or sharp and focused?"
2. **Commendation** - "Who gets the Officer of the Sprint badge? Name them and their act of heroism."
3. **Overheard in the Precinct** - "What's the most memorable thing someone said this sprint? The quote that should go in the case file."

**Facilitator action:** Collect and note down the answers (e.g., in a personal notepad or scratch area). Do NOT fill template sections yet - that happens during the writing phase.

---

## Phase 3: Individual Writing (0:12-0:24, 12 min)

### Prompts:

- "If an investigator followed our team this sprint, what case would they build?"
- "What evidence from this sprint tells the real story?"
- "What happened this sprint that needs to go on the record?"
- "When did you crack the case, and when did the trail go cold?"
- "What case from this sprint should make it into the department's hall of fame?"

> Each person picks one or more sections to fill: Case Summary, Witness Statement, Forensic Analysis, Suspect Profile, Cold Cases, Most Wanted, or Sketch Artist.

### Facilitator actions:

- **Fill ice breaker sections now** - Transfer your notes from Phase 2 into the Precinct Morale, Commendations, and Overheard in the Precinct sections on the board
- "Detectives, 5 minutes before we convene in the briefing room."
- "2 minutes. Wrap up your reports."
- Use this time to polish your own sections (Case Statistics, Morale, Commendations, Quote) and tidy formatting on the board

---

## Phase 4: Gallery Walk (0:24-0:29, 5 min)

> "Reports are in. Now walk the evidence board. Read each file silently. Look for connections between cases."

---

## Phase 5: Sharing (0:29-0:40, 11 min)

Each person gets ~1.5 minutes.

Stay in character: "Interesting lead, detective. Tell us more about the evidence." or "That's a strong suspect. What's the motive?"

---

## Phase 6: Discussion (0:40-0:50, 10 min)

Pick 2-3:

**On the case:**
- "Which case surprised you most? Any connections between reports?"
- "If we had to present this case to the commissioner (management), what's the headline?"
- "Did any witnesses contradict each other? What does that tell us?"

**On the investigation:**
- "What evidence are we missing? Where should we dig deeper?"
- "Is there a cold case we keep ignoring? Why?"
- "What was our biggest investigative mistake this sprint?"

**Looking ahead:**
- "What case should we prioritize next sprint?"
- "What's on the Most Wanted board for the next two weeks?"

---

## Phase 7: Closing (0:50-0:55, 5 min)

> "Case closed, detectives. Outstanding work. The precinct is proud. Now get some rest - new cases hit the desk in two weeks."

- Capture 1-3 action items
- **Optional:** Collect exit mood (compare with opening Precinct Morale)
- **Optional:** Vote for "Detective of the Sprint"

---

## Post-Retro

- Compile all reports into a case file
- Share: "CASE CLOSED: Sprint [XX] - Full report available in [channel]."

---

## When to Use This Format

This template works especially well for:
- Sprints with significant bugs or incidents
- Post-mortem sessions
- Sprints where root cause analysis is important
- Teams that need to address recurring problems
- When you want to investigate without assigning blame
