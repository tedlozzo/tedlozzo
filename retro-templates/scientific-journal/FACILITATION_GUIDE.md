# The Scientific Journal - Facilitation Guide

**Facilitator Role:** Lab Director
**Duration:** 55 minutes
**Format:** Academic journal with research papers, lab notes, and experimental data

---

## Preparation (~10 min before the retro)

1. **Choose the theme** - Pick a scientific discipline or journal style that fits the sprint mood (e.g., "Quantum Sprint Review," "Field Biology Report," "Clinical Trials Weekly")
2. **Prepare the template on the whiteboard** - Import the SVG template, lock it so participants cannot accidentally move elements
3. **Pre-fill Lab Metrics** - Pull sprint stats from the Sprint Review (velocity, planned vs delivered, bugs, carry-over) and fill in the "Lab Metrics" section
4. **Pre-fill date and volume info** - Sprint number, date range, journal volume/issue number
5. **Review section ownership** - Know which sections you fill, which the team fills together, and which participants fill individually (see below)

---

## Setup

- Print SVG templates (one per participant) OR set up digital whiteboard with locked template
- Markers, pens
- Timer visible to all
- Optional: lab coat for facilitator, "safety goggles" prop

---

## Section Ownership

### Facilitator fills before the retro (from Sprint Review)
- **Lab Metrics** - Sprint velocity, planned vs delivered, story points, bugs found/fixed
- **Date / Volume info** - Sprint number, date range, journal volume and issue number

### Facilitator fills during writing phase (from ice breaker data)
- **Lab Conditions** - Mood collected from participants (Phase 3)
- **Lab Safety Award** - Kudos nominated by participants (Phase 3)
- **Abstract / Quote** - One-sentence findings collected from participants (Phase 3)

### Team fills together
- **Journal Name** - Chosen collaboratively during Phase 1

### Participants fill individually
- **Breakthrough Discovery** - Main achievement or finding
- **Peer Review** - Feedback on something the team did
- **Lab Notes** - Technical observations worth documenting
- **Hypothesis vs Reality** - What you predicted vs what happened
- **Side Effects** - Unintended consequences noticed
- **Classified Research** - Things that need private follow-up or sensitive topics

---

## Phase 1: Set the Stage (0:00-0:05, 5 min)

### Lab Director Script:

> "Good [morning/afternoon], researchers. Welcome to the Sprint Laboratory.
>
> This sprint has been a series of experiments, and it's time to publish our findings. Each of you will write one contribution for our journal. You can write:
> - A **breakthrough discovery** (your main finding or achievement)
> - A **peer review** (feedback on something the team did)
> - **Lab notes** (technical observations worth documenting)
> - A **hypothesis vs reality** report (what you predicted vs what happened)
> - A **side effects** report (unintended consequences you noticed)
>
> I've already filled in our Lab Metrics from the Sprint Review. Now - what shall we name our journal?"

Let team choose a journal name. Briefly explain section ownership: which parts you will fill, which they will fill individually.

---

## Phase 2: Ice Breakers (0:05-0:12, 7 min)

Collect answers from ALL participants. The facilitator **notes down** the responses but does **not** fill template sections yet -- that happens during the writing phase.

1. **Lab Conditions** - "What's the atmospheric pressure in your lab right now? Stable, volatile, high-pressure, or calm?" -- note each person's mood.
2. **Lab Safety Award** - "Name one person who kept the lab safe this sprint - who caught something before it blew up?" -- note nominations.
3. **Abstract** - "In one sentence, what was the key finding of your sprint?" -- note each sentence.

---

## Phase 3: Individual Writing (0:12-0:24, 12 min)

### Prompts to share:

- "If a researcher studied our team this sprint, what would their key finding be?"
- "What experiment from this sprint produced surprising results?"
- "What data from this sprint matters most?"
- "When did you feel most like a breakthrough was happening, or most stuck in a dead end?"
- "What discovery would you present at a conference?"

### Facilitator actions:

- Start timer, give 5-min and 2-min warnings
- "Researchers, peer review deadline approaching!"
- **Fill ice breaker sections now** from your Phase 2 notes: Lab Conditions (mood summary), Lab Safety Award (kudos), and Abstract/Quote (one-sentence findings)
- Use remaining time to polish your own sections (Lab Metrics formatting, Lab Conditions visualization)

---

## Phase 4: Gallery Walk (0:24-0:29, 5 min)

> "Submissions closed. Now let's do a peer review walk. Read each paper silently. Take mental notes on what surprises you."

---

## Phase 5: Sharing (0:29-0:40, 11 min)

Each person gets ~1-1.5 minutes to present their paper.

The level of character commitment is up to the facilitator's taste -- from light flavor ("Nice finding!") to full immersion ("Fascinating findings, doctor. What was your sample size?" or "Interesting methodology.").

---

## Phase 6: Discussion (0:40-0:50, 10 min)

Pick 2-3:

**On findings:**
- "Which discovery surprised you most?"
- "If you had to award a Nobel Prize for this sprint, who gets it and why?"
- "Did any two papers contradict each other? What does that tell us?"

**On methodology:**
- "What experiment should we run differently next time?"
- "Where did our methodology fail us this sprint?"
- "What's the experiment we keep avoiding?"

**Looking ahead:**
- "What hypothesis do we want to test next sprint?"
- "What research grant would you apply for?"

---

## Phase 7: Closing (0:50-0:55, 5 min)

> "And that concludes this edition of our journal. Remember: every sprint is a new experiment. The failed ones teach us the most. Back to the lab."

Capture 1-3 action items.

**Optional closing activities (pick one):**
- **Exit mood** - "How's your lab atmosphere now compared to when we started?"
- **One-word finding** - Each person shares one word that captures their sprint
- **Vote for Best Paper** - Team votes on the most insightful contribution

---

## Post-Retro

- Compile all papers into a "journal issue"
- Share: "New issue published! [Journal Name], Vol. [X]"
