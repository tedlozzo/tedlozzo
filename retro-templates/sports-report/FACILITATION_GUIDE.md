# The Sports Report - Facilitation Guide

**Facilitator Role:** Sports Commentator / Pundit
**Duration:** 55 minutes
**Format:** Match report with highlights, tactical analysis, and player profiles
**Note:** How much you lean into the commentator character is entirely up to your taste — go full pundit, keep it light, or stay mostly straight-faced. Whatever works for your team.

---

## Preparation (~10 min before the retro)

1. **Choose a theme** - Pick a sport or league flavor for this retro (football, basketball, F1, etc.) or keep it generic
2. **Prepare the template on the whiteboard** - Import the SVG template, then lock it so participants cannot accidentally move or edit the base layout
3. **Pre-fill sprint metrics** - Pull key numbers from the Sprint Review and fill in the "Game Statistics" section (velocity, stories completed, bugs fixed, etc.)
4. **Fill match date and fixture info** - Sprint name/number, dates, teams or squads involved
5. **Review section ownership** - Confirm which sections you will fill vs. what the team fills (see below)

---

## Setup

- Print SVG templates (one per participant) OR set up digital whiteboard
- Markers, pens
- Timer visible to all
- Optional: whistle, commentator microphone prop, sports jersey

---

## Section Ownership

### Facilitator fills before retro (from Sprint Review)
- **Game Statistics** - Sprint stats (velocity, capacity, stories done, bugs, etc.)
- **Match date / fixture info** - Sprint name, dates, teams

### Facilitator fills during writing phase (from ice breaker data)
- **Pitch Conditions** - Mood collected from the team (Phase 2), filled in template (Phase 3)
- **Fan Mail / MVP** - Kudos collected from the team (Phase 2), filled in template (Phase 3)
- **Post-Match Quote** - One-liners collected from the team (Phase 2), filled in template (Phase 3)

### Team fills together
- **Team name and war cry** - Decided collaboratively during Set the Stage

### Participants fill individually
- **Match Report** - The full story of the sprint
- **Highlight Reel** - Top moments
- **Tactical Analysis** - What worked and what didn't strategically
- **Player Profile** - Spotlight on a teammate
- **Injury Report** - What blocked us
- **Transfer Rumors** - What skill, tool, or knowledge do we need to recruit

---

## Phase 1: Set the Stage (0:00-0:05, 5 min)

### Commentator Script:

> "Ladies and gentlemen, welcome to the Sprint Arena! What a match we've had over the past two weeks!
>
> I'm your commentator, and we're here to break down everything that happened on the pitch. Each of you is a sports reporter covering a different angle. You can write:
> - A **match report** (the full story of the sprint)
> - A **highlight reel** (the top moments)
> - A **tactical analysis** (what worked and what didn't strategically)
> - A **player profile** (spotlight on a teammate)
> - An **injury report** (what blocked us)
>
> But first - what's our team name and war cry?"

### Facilitator actions:
- Let the team pick a name and war cry, fill it in on the board
- Briefly explain section ownership: "I've already filled in the stats from Sprint Review. During the next round I'll capture your mood and shout-outs. After that, you'll each fill your own reporter sections."

---

## Phase 2: Ice Breakers (0:05-0:12, 7 min)

Collect from ALL participants. Facilitator **only notes down** the responses (notebook, sticky notes, or a scratch area on the board) — do NOT fill the template sections yet.

1. **Pitch Conditions** - "What was the pitch like for you this sprint? Electric, muddy, frozen, or perfect conditions?"
2. **Fan Mail** - "Write a fan letter to a teammate. Who was your MVP this sprint?"
3. **Post-Match Quote** - "If the press interviewed you after this sprint, what's your one-liner?"

---

## Phase 3: Individual Writing (0:12-0:24, 12 min)

### Prompts:

- "If a sports reporter covered our sprint, what would the headline be?"
- "What play from this sprint deserves to be in the highlight reel?"
- "What was the turning point of the match?"
- "When did you feel most like we were winning, or most like we were behind?"
- "What moment would make the crowd roar?"

### Facilitator actions:

- **Fill the ice breaker sections now** from your notes: Pitch Conditions, Fan Mail / MVP, and Post-Match Quotes
- While the team writes, polish your own sections (Game Statistics formatting, Pitch Conditions summary, Fan Mail layout, Post-Match Quotes)
- "Second half underway, reporters! 5 minutes to filing deadline!"
- "Last minute! Wrap up your reports!"

---

## Phase 4: Gallery Walk (0:24-0:29, 5 min)

> "Full time whistle! Now walk the press box and read your colleagues' reports. Scouts honor - no talking yet."

---

## Phase 5: Sharing (0:29-0:40, 11 min)

Each person gets ~1.5 minutes.

Stay in character: "What a goal! Take us through the buildup." or "Tough tackle there. Walk us through the injury."

---

## Phase 6: Discussion (0:40-0:50, 10 min)

Pick 2-3:

**On the match:**
- "What was the goal of the tournament? Did we score?"
- "If you could replay one moment differently, what would it be?"
- "Which highlight reel clip would go viral?"

**On tactics:**
- "What formation should we run next sprint?"
- "Where did we leave gaps in defense?"
- "Was there an unexpected assist that changed the game?"

**Looking ahead:**
- "What's our game plan for the next fixture?"
- "What player do we need to recruit (skill, tool, knowledge)?"

---

## Phase 7: Closing (0:50-0:55, 5 min)

> "And that's the final whistle! What a sprint it's been. Hit the showers, recover, and get ready for the next fixture. Same time, same pitch."

### Facilitator actions:

- Capture 1-3 action items
- **Optional:** Quick exit mood check - "Thumbs up / sideways / down: how are you feeling heading into the next sprint?"
- **Optional:** Vote for "Player of the Sprint" - team nominates and votes on who stood out this sprint

---

## Post-Retro

- Compile into match report
- Share: "FULL TIME REPORT: [Team] [Score] - [Backlog] [Score]!"
